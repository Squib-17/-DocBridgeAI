# Personal Loan Payments Guide

## Payment Overview

Your NextGen Capital personal loan has a fixed monthly payment that stays the same for the entire loan term. This guide covers everything you need to know about making payments.

| Payment Method | Processing Time | Cutoff Time | Fee |
|----------------|-----------------|-------------|-----|
| Autopay | Same day | 5:00 PM ET | Free |
| Online (Bank Account) | Same day | 5:00 PM ET | Free |
| Online (Debit Card) | Same day | 11:59 PM ET | Free |
| Mobile App | Same day | 5:00 PM ET | Free |
| Phone (Automated) | Same day | 5:00 PM ET | Free |
| Phone (Agent) | Same day | 5:00 PM ET | Free |
| Mail (Check/Money Order) | 3-5 business days | N/A | Free |

## Autopay

### Why Autopay is Recommended

Autopay is the best way to manage your personal loan payment:
- **Never miss a payment** - automated deduction on your due date
- **Rate discount** - 0.25% APR reduction when enrolled (applied retroactively to first payment)
- **Peace of mind** - one less thing to remember each month

### Autopay Details

| Setting | How It Works |
|---------|--------------|
| Amount | Fixed - always your scheduled monthly payment |
| Date | Your payment due date (or up to 5 days before if you choose) |
| Source | Your linked bank account |
| Changes | Can change bank account anytime; date changes once per year |

**Note:** Unlike credit cards, personal loan autopay is always for the fixed payment amount. There's no "pay extra" autopay option - extra payments must be made separately.

### Setting Up Autopay

**During Loan Application:**
- Select autopay during the application process
- 0.25% rate discount applied to your final APR

**After Loan Funding:**
1. Log in to your account
2. Go to "Payments" → "Set Up Autopay"
3. Link a bank account (if not already linked)
4. Select payment date (due date or earlier)
5. Confirm enrollment

**Autopay Rate Discount:**
- If enrolled within 30 days of funding: Discount applied from first payment
- If enrolled later: Discount applies starting next payment
- If you cancel autopay: Discount is removed, rate increases by 0.25%

### Autopay Failures

If autopay fails:
1. We notify you immediately (push, email, SMS)
2. We retry the payment once within 2 business days
3. If the retry fails, autopay is paused (not canceled)
4. Make a manual payment to avoid late fee
5. Resolve the issue with your bank
6. Autopay resumes the following month

**Common Failure Reasons:**
- Insufficient funds
- Bank account closed or frozen
- Bank rejected the ACH transaction
- Account number changed

**See OPS-002 for detailed autopay failure handling procedures.**

### Making Extra Payments

Extra payments help you pay off your loan faster and save on interest.

**How Extra Payments Work:**

Personal loan payments are applied in this order:
1. Accrued interest (to date)
2. Principal balance
3. Any fees owed

When you make an extra payment, it goes toward principal (after covering any accrued interest), which:
- Reduces your total balance
- Decreases future interest charges
- Can shorten your loan term

**Extra Payment Options:**

| Option | How to Select |
|--------|---------------|
| Reduce Future Payments | Principal reduces; same number of payments but lower amounts |
| Shorten Loan Term | Same payment amounts; fewer payments (DEFAULT) |

By default, extra payments shorten your loan term (you finish paying sooner). If you prefer lower future payments, contact us to change this setting.

## Payment Due Date

### How Your Due Date Works

Your payment due date is the same day each month, set when your loan was funded.

**Example Timeline:**
- Loan funded: January 5
- First payment due: February 5
- All future payments due: 5th of each month

### Changing Your Due Date

You can request a due date change **once per 12 months**:

**Eligible Dates:** Any day from the 1st to the 28th

**How to Request:**
1. Call 1-800-NEXTGEN, OR
2. Submit request in app under "Account Settings"

**Important Notes:**
- Change takes effect the following payment
- One payment period may be shorter or longer than usual
- Interest accrues daily, so changing the date affects that month's interest slightly

### Weekends and Holidays

If your due date falls on a weekend or federal holiday:
- Payment is due by the **next business day**
- No late fee if paid by 5:00 PM ET on that day

**Example:**
- Due date: Saturday, March 15
- Payment actually due: Monday, March 17
- No late fee if paid by Monday 5:00 PM ET

## How Your Payment is Applied

### Amortization Explained

Personal loans use **amortization**, meaning each payment covers both interest and principal, but the mix changes over time.

**Early Payments:** More goes to interest
**Later Payments:** More goes to principal

**Example - $15,000 loan at 12% for 48 months ($395/month):**

| Payment # | To Interest | To Principal | Balance After |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|---------------|
| 1 | $150.00 | $245.00 | $14,755.00 |
| 12 | $127.65 | $267.35 | $12,011.23 |
| 24 | $99.45 | $295.55 | $8,649.38 |
| 36 | $66.26 | $328.74 | $4,704.25 |
| 48 | $3.92 | $391.08 | $0.00 |

### Daily Interest Accrual

Interest accrues daily on your outstanding principal balance:

**Daily Interest = (Principal Balance × APR) ÷ 365**

**Example:**
- Balance: $10,000
- APR: 12%
- Daily interest: ($10,000 × 0.12) ÷ 365 = $3.29 per day

This is why paying early or making extra payments saves money - you reduce the balance that's accruing daily interest.

## Paying Off Your Loan

### Payoff Quote

To get your exact payoff amount:
1. **Online:** Log in → "Loan Details" → "Request Payoff Quote"
2. **Phone:** Call 1-800-NEXTGEN

**Payoff Quote Includes:**
- Current principal balance
- Interest accrued to the payoff date
- Any outstanding fees
- Good-through date (usually 10 days)

**Important:** Payoff amounts change daily due to interest accrual. Always get a fresh quote before making a final payment.

### Making Your Final Payment

**Standard Payoff:**
1. Request payoff quote
2. Make payment for exact payoff amount
3. Ensure payment is received by the quote's good-through date
4. Account closes automatically

**Overpayment:**
If you pay more than the payoff amount, we'll refund the difference:
- Refunds under $1 are not issued (unless requested)
- Refunds over $1 are mailed within 7 business days
- Refunds over $50 can be direct deposited (call to arrange)

### After Payoff

Within 30 days of your final payment:
- You'll receive a paid-in-full letter
- Credit bureaus will be updated (account paid in full)
- Any security interest is released (if applicable)
- Account will show closed on your credit report

## Partial Payments

### What Happens with Partial Payments

If you pay less than your scheduled amount:
- Payment is applied to accrued interest first, then principal
- Remaining amount becomes past due
- Late fee may apply if minimum not met by due date
- Next payment due will include the shortage

**Example:**
- Payment due: $400
- You pay: $250
- Past due amount: $150
- Next payment due: $400 (regular) + $150 (past due) = $550

### Avoiding Default

If you can only make partial payments, contact us immediately. Options may include:
- Payment arrangement
- Due date change
- Hardship program enrollment

It's always better to contact us than to simply pay less without communication.

## Biweekly Payments

### How Biweekly Payments Work

Instead of one monthly payment, you pay half your payment every two weeks.

**Benefits:**
- 26 half-payments per year = 13 full payments (one extra)
- Reduces loan term significantly
- Saves on total interest

**Example:**
- Monthly payment: $400
- Biweekly payment: $200 every two weeks
- Annual payments: 26 × $200 = $5,200 (vs. 12 × $400 = $4,800)
- Extra $400/year goes directly to principal

### Enrolling in Biweekly Payments

1. Call 1-800-NEXTGEN to enroll
2. Set up deduction from your bank account
3. Payments deducted every other Friday
4. Can switch back to monthly at any time

**Note:** If autopay discount applied, it remains with biweekly payments.

## Troubleshooting

### Payment Not Reflected

If your payment doesn't appear:
1. Verify the payment date has passed
2. Check your bank account for the debit
3. Confirm you used the correct account number
4. Contact us with your confirmation number

Processing times:
- Online payments: Same day (if before cutoff)
- Mailed payments: 3-5 business days after receipt

### Wrong Payment Amount Debited

If the wrong amount was debited:
1. Note the discrepancy (how much was expected vs. actual)
2. Contact us immediately at 1-800-NEXTGEN
3. We'll investigate and correct within 3 business days
4. Any fees caused by our error will be reversed

### Bank Account Changed

If your bank account closed or changed:
1. Log in and remove the old account
2. Add your new bank account
3. Update autopay (if enrolled)
4. Make a manual payment if due date is approaching
