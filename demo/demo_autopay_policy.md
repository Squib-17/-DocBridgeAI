Autopay Enrollment Policy for Retail Banking Customers

This document outlines the terms and conditions governing the autopay enrollment service offered to retail banking customers. The autopay service allows eligible acct holders to authorize automatic pymnts from a designated funding source on a recurring basis. All custs who wish to participate in the autopay program must meet the eligibility requirements set forth in this policy and must agree to the terms described herein before enrollment can be confirmed.

To be eligible for autopay enrollment, a cust must hold an active acct in good standing with no pending collections activity, no unresolved chargebacks, and no active fraud investigations. The acct must have been open for a minimum of 90 days prior to the enrollment request. The cust must have a verified email address and a valid U.S. phone number on file for notification purposes. Accts that have been frozen, suspended, or flagged for review by the security compliance unit are not eligible for autopay enrollment until the underlying issue has been fully resolved and the acct has been reinstated to current status.

The enrollment process begins when the cust logs into their online banking portal or contacts the customer service team via phone or chat. The cust must select the pymnt amount type, which may be the full statement bal, the minimum pymnt due, or a fixed dollar amount not less than the minimum pymnt due. The cust must also designate the funding source, which must be an external bank acct that has been verified through the standard micro-deposit verification process or an instant verification partner. Once the enrollment form is submitted and confirmed, the first autopymnt will be initiated on the next scheduled pymnt due date provided the enrollment is completed at least three business days before that date.

If enrollment is completed fewer than three business days before the scheduled pymnt due date, the first autopymnt will be deferred to the following billing cycle and the cust will be responsible for making the current pymnt manually to avoid late fees. The cust will receive a confirmation email at the verified address on file within 24 hours of a successful enrollment. If no confirmation email is received within 48 hours, the cust should contact customer service to verify the enrollment status before assuming the enrollment was successful.

Autopymnts are processed on the pymnt due date each billing cycle unless that date falls on a weekend or federal banking holiday, in which case the pymnt will be processed on the next available business day. The cust should ensure that sufficient funds are available in the designated funding acct on the scheduled processing date. If the autopymnt fails due to insufficient funds, the cust will be assessed a returned pymnt fee as described in the fee schedule section of the account agreement. A single returned pymnt in a 12-month period may qualify for a one-time courtesy waiver of the returned pymnt fee at the discretion of the customer service representative or their manager. Repeated returned pymnts may result in automatic disenrollment from the autopay program and additional risk review of the acct.

The pymnt amount for autopay will reflect the chosen pymnt type as of the date the pymnt is processed. If the cust has selected minimum pymnt due, the amount processed will be the minimum pymnt shown on the most recent statement. If the cust has selected full statement bal, the amount processed will be the full bal shown on the most recent statement. In both cases the amount may differ from prior billing cycles based on purchases, credits, and fees applied to the acct during the intervening period. The cust is encouraged to review each monthly statement to understand the amount that will be processed by autopay before the pymnt date.

Changes to the autopay enrollment, including modifications to the pymnt amount type, the pymnt date, or the designated funding source, may be made at any time through the online banking portal or by contacting customer service. Changes submitted fewer than three business days before the next scheduled autopymnt may not take effect until the following billing cycle. Cancellation of the autopay enrollment may also be requested at any time and will take effect no later than three business days after the request is received. The cust remains responsible for any pymnts that were already scheduled and in process at the time the cancellation request is submitted.

The autopay service is subject to the general terms and conditions of the account agreement. The bank reserves the right to modify, suspend, or discontinue the autopay service at any time with reasonable notice to enrolled custs. In the event of a system outage or technical failure that prevents a scheduled autopymnt from processing on time, the bank will not assess late fees for that billing cycle provided the cust notifies customer service within 10 business days of the missed pymnt. Custs who believe an autopymnt was processed in error should contact customer service immediately to initiate a review. The bank will investigate and issue any applicable credits within three to five business days of receiving the complaint.
